## Group-Stage Fixtures — 12 Groups Of Four

The group phase is where the tournament opens up. With **12 groups (A through L)**, each containing four nations, the early <a href="#">World Cup 2026 group fixtures</a> deliver a steady wave of three-match mini-leagues playing out in parallel across all 16 host cities.

Every group runs the same **round-robin** pattern. Below is the template each of the twelve groups follows — the same match-day structure repeats twelve times over.

| Group Match-Day | Fixture Pattern |
|:---|:---|
| Match-Day 1 | Team 1 vs Team 2 · Team 3 vs Team 4 |
| Match-Day 2 | Team 1 vs Team 3 · Team 2 vs Team 4 |
| Match-Day 3 | Team 1 vs Team 4 · Team 2 vs Team 3 |

**Key group-stage points to know:**
- **Six fixtures per group**, played over three match-days.
- **The final round of each group is scheduled simultaneously** to keep qualification fair.
- **Top two go through automatically**, giving 24 guaranteed qualifiers.
- **The eight best third-placed teams** fill the remaining knockout spots — a new wrinkle created by the 12-group format.

This third-place mechanism is unique to the expanded <a href="#">World Cup 2026 format</a>. Because 12 group runners-up would only supply 24 teams for a 32-team bracket, FIFA ranks all twelve third-placed sides and promotes the best eight, so even a third-place finish can keep your nation alive.

**How the bracket flows:**

The knockout path is where the <a href="#">World Cup 2026 knockout fixtures</a> tension peaks: a single result ends a nation's tournament, so the margins that felt comfortable in the group stage vanish entirely.
